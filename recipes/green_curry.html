Thai Green Curry

Spicy, super flavorful, tastes like a million bucks!

Lights fireworks filled with flavor in your mouth without the danger factor of actual fireworks! Wanting to try something new but aren't wanting to break the bank? Try this simple recipe.

If the dish is too spicy, add a little more sugar to take the bite away. Personally, I crave spicy things. It makes me feel alive.

Ingredients

For the crispy tofu:

  • 1 14oz. block extra firm tofu
  • 1 tbsp low-sodium tamari
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast

For the green curry:

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 4-5 tbsp green curry paste
  • 1 13.5oz can full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp coconut sugar
  • 3 cups mixed vegetables (such as peppers, pumpkin, bamboo shoots, and/or eggplant)
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425F. Cut the pressed tofu block in half lengthwise, then cut each rectangle into 16 even cubes.
  2. Add the cubed tofu to a large bowl. Pour the tamari over the tofu, then use a spatula to gently toss until evenly coated. Add in the nutritional yeast and repeat.
  3. Transfer the tofu to a baking sheet lined with a silicone mat, leaving space between each piece. Bake on the top rack of the oven for 20 minutes, then carefully flip and bake for another 20-25 minutes. In the meantime, prepare the green curry.
  4. Bring a nonstick pan to medium heat and add the coconut oil. Once melted, add in the green curry paste; fry the paste for 90 seconds, to bring out the flavors.
  5. Add the coconut milk and coconut sugar to the pan and mix until well combined. Add in the mixed vegetables, stir well, and bring the heat to high. Once the mixture starts to bubble, reduce the heat to medium-low and let simmer, covered, for 15-17 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Place some cooked rice into each serving bowl, then top with green curry and crispy tofu. Serve warm.
